Study 01 · AI visibility field note

Four AI assistants. Four different local recommendations.

Across four Arizona cities, the clearest finding was not a single winner. It was how differently AI assistants can see the same local market.

Updated July 2026 · Nano Solutions

BrightLocal's Local Consumer Review Survey 2026 found that 45% of consumers use generative AI tools for local-business recommendations. Yet recommendation slots remain selective: SOCi's 2026 Local Visibility Index found that ChatGPT recommended only 1.2% of brand locations across 2,751 multi-location brands.

Nano Solutions recorded a point-in-time comparison of public AI recommendations across Gilbert, Chandler, Mesa, and Tempe in July 2026. Supporting records are retained internally.

One market, four different answers

Gilbert produced the clearest example: four major AI assistants surfaced four different businesses as the top local recommendation.

The important finding was not a winner

Across the broader four-city observation, overlap varied. Some markets showed clearer agreement, while Gilbert produced none at the top. For a local-business owner, that means the assistant a customer chooses can materially change which companies enter the consideration set.

AI assistants do not assemble local recommendations in exactly the same way. In this observation, their answers reflected different mixes of public information and platform-specific judgment. A strong position in traditional search — or in one assistant — did not guarantee visibility everywhere else.

SOCi found the same kind of disconnect at scale in retail: only 45% of the most visible enterprise brands in traditional local search overlapped with those most frequently recommended by AI platforms. Search visibility and AI visibility are related, but they are not the same scorecard.

What this means for your business

  • Visibility is platform-specific. Appearing in one assistant does not guarantee that another will mention you.
  • Your competitive set can change. The business beside you in one answer may be absent from the next.
  • A current diagnosis matters. Generic checklists miss market-specific gaps and changing platform behavior.

Paid placement observed in one product did not create consistent visibility across the others. There is no single switch that controls every AI recommendation.

Diagnosis beats a generic checklist

There is no universal recipe for becoming an AI recommendation. Priorities depend on the market, the competing businesses, and the visibility gaps found in a current audit. The useful output is a clear, evidence-based order of operations — not a promise of a permanent ranking.

AI answers change, and anyone promising “#1 on ChatGPT” is selling certainty that does not exist. What can be measured is where a business appears today, where it does not, and what deserves attention first.

Evidence & limitations
