Privacy · Plainly stated

Anonymous measurement. Personal details kept out of it.

Nano Solutions measures aggregate site activity and uses the information you deliberately submit only to respond to your request.

Effective July 14, 2026

What the site measures

Nano Solutions uses Umami, a privacy-focused analytics service, to understand aggregate website use. It records page visits, anonymous interactions such as calls to action or sample downloads, and limited technical context such as referrer, browser, operating system, device type, and country.

The tracker is configured to use no cookies, respect a browser's Do Not Track setting, and exclude URL query strings and page fragments. Nano Solutions does not send names, email addresses, business details, form contents, or other submitted field values to analytics.

What happens when you submit the form

The free-check form asks for your name, business name, market, website, and email address. Those details are sent through FormSubmit to the Nano Solutions inbox so the request can be assessed and answered.

Submitted information is used to respond to the request and provide any service you choose to purchase. It is not added to a newsletter or sold. FormSubmit states that form submissions may be retained by its service for up to 30 days; its current terms are available in its privacy document.

Your choices

You can enable Do Not Track in your browser to prevent Nano Solutions analytics from recording the visit. You can also block JavaScript without losing access to the site's core content or contact form.

To ask about, correct, or delete information you submitted directly to Nano Solutions, email nanosolutionshq@gmail.com.

Scope of this notice

This page explains the current website setup in plain language. It is not a substitute for jurisdiction-specific legal advice. If the site's measurement or form provider changes, this notice will be updated.
